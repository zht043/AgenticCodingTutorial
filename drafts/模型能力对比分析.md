These models evolve rapidly, so capabilities can shift with updates.

### Popularity in Various Application Scenarios

Popularity varies by use case, driven by factors like cost, integration, benchmarks, and user preferences. From recent data and user discussions:

- **General Chat and Professional Work**: GPT series leads in adoption due to versatility and familiarity, followed by Claude for thoughtful reasoning and Gemini for speed/integration. GPT is the most widely used overall, with high subscriber rates. Chinese models like Qwen and GLM gain traction in multilingual/Asian markets.

- **Coding and Software Engineering**: Claude Opus is highly favored for precision and complex tasks, with Grok and DeepSeek popular for efficiency and math-heavy coding. GLM and Kimi are rising alternatives for cost savings.

- **Agentic and Automation Tasks**: Grok and Kimi stand out for multi-agent/multi-tool workflows, while Claude and GPT are preferred for reliability. Minimax is gaining for low-latency agents.

- **Multimodal and Data Processing**: Gemini dominates for long-context and visual tasks, with GPT close behind. Qwen is popular for image/composition in creative apps.

- **Cost-Efficient/Open-Source Scenarios**: DeepSeek, Qwen, and Kimi are most popular for budget builds, especially in startups and research. Grok appeals for unfiltered, high-performance value.

Overall, no single model dominates universally—users often stack multiple for optimal results, with Western models (GPT, Claude, Gemini) leading global popularity, while Chinese ones (DeepSeek, Kimi, etc.) surge in efficiency-focused and Asian applications.
